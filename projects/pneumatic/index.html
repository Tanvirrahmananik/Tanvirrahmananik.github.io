Pneumatic

A Pneumatic Circuit Project using Festo Components

Just wrapped up a pneumatic circuit project using Festo components.

The system runs a repeating A+ B+ B– A– sequence, controlled by a selector switch and backed by an emergency stop for added safety.

It’s always satisfying to see simple pneumatic logic deliver reliable and repeatable motion without needing complex programming. Festo valves and cylinders made the setup smooth and responsive.

These kinds of solutions remind me why pneumatics are still a great choice for many industrial tasks—clean, fast, and easy to maintain.

Happy to connect with others working on similar systems!

Pneumatic Timers & Counters, Actuators, Switches

Pneumatic Timers & Counters – Still a Core Skill in Automation
This week, in our hands-on lab, we revisited two foundational yet often overlooked devices:

1. Pneumatic Timer
A purely mechanical device that introduces a time delay without electricity. Ideal for:

  • Delay ON/OFF functions
  • Hazardous/explosive environments
  • Teaching time-delay logic without PLCs

2. Pneumatic Counter with Gate Function
A mechanical counter that registers air pulses and resets after reaching a preset value. The gate input controls when the counter is active — an excellent concept for introducing conditional logic and signal flow.

What I love about teaching this topic is that it encourages students to think logically and understand the physical behavior of automation systems — before jumping into code.

Despite all the advancements in PLCs, HMIs, and SCADA, these basic pneumatic components still serve in packaging lines, safety-critical zones, and cost-sensitive installations.
